Who Am I?
‘One and only’ might seem like a bold claim until you’re talking about someone with an eleven syllable name. In all seriousness, however, I, Dylan Patrick David Bebbington Sheary, started playing the piano eleven years ago with Heather Bolton & Key To The Keys. Since then, I’ve experimented with songs of my own design, with very mixed success at best, until I came up with one idea I thought I could take further:
I heard two simple notes whistled once a few minutes after getting up, in early 2020. The melodic pair stuck in my head that day, for it felt like the pair were the beginning of something more, something grand.
Rather than moving onto learning a new song in my piano lesson that night, I thought I’d ask about booting up Garageband on my Teacher’s Mac and seeing if I could put that idea together.
Later that night, I created the first draft save of Starlit Odyssey, and began a musical journey over the next full year of my life that changed it for the better.

Ever since then, I’ve promised myself each mote of potential of a unique idea deserved to be, at the very least, recorded. Today, there are 62 voice recordings of whistlings of vague ideas, each of them with the same potential as the two notes I heard one morning only to develop them into Starlit Odyssey.

I’m just as excited as you should be to see where my musical path leads me next.
The composers I’ve listened to the most that have inspired what I really want out of my own music are Rhian Sheehan (World famous in New Zealand, of course), Adam Young, Epic Mountain, Justin E. Bell, Slow Meadow, C418, Thomas Barrandon, and Andrew Prahlow.
Always keep an ear out for anything that can inspire you, I never knew where listening to these brilliant musicians would take me but today I can say it was well worth every minute.
